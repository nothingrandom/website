You're best at being you

Date

2nd March, 2015

Reading time

2 minutes

Category

Personal

It's cliche, but the best person at being you - is you.

The last couple of months

I'd like to think I've changed a lot recently. For the time being education is over, and I'm now working full time. I started with a 3 month internship at Built by Buffalo in September. From there, I moved on to working full time with the team at Metropoly. Alongside that, I'm working on a mix of personal projects and freelance side projects. I'm also beginning to get more heavily involved in live sound engineering. I've become busier, and I've learnt a lot. There is still a lot to learn though.

Self acceptance

This is something that everyone struggles with. Working out who you are, and then actually accepting that. As we walk through life, we encounter many hurdles. I find it odd that our biggest hurdle is often ourself. This is something that I've recently been working through.

"Finding myself", learning what I like and what I don't. Trying to work out what I want in life. One of the biggest stumbling blocks, is listening to what other people think of you. Believing you have to dress, speak and act a certain way, hang out with a group of people, or listen to a specific type of music in order to be accepted by society.

At the end of the day, what matters more? What others think of you, or making sure you're happy?

I'm slowly moving myself out of this box that I've placed myself in. Stepping outside of my comfort zone, and doing things because I enjoy them. Not because I want people to accept me.

Let's be honest here, if someone doesn't accept you for being you - are they worth changing for?
